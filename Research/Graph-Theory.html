Lectures and Papers

  1. Matching algorithms
    Includes augmenting path, Hopcroft-Karp algorithm, Edmonds method. Algorithms and proofs as well as the definition and lemma of blossom is more clear.
  2. Graph matching
  3. nonbipartite matching
    Includes Edmonds method, explains why bolssom shirking is needed in general graph.
    • In DFS, a node may be encountered both in odd and even position. It’s necessary to record both of the two kinds of visited label. Otherwise, feasible augmenting path may exists but not found.
    • However, if the algorithm distinguish the two kinds of visited label, odd length cycle may exists in a found path. Therefore, blossom shirking is needed.
